The old ways approach

High-welfare farming that mimics nature

'The Old Ways' practises high-welfare farming systems which mimic nature and improve biodiversity and soil health. Each week their hens are moved to fresh pasture with free-range to roam and forage.

Weekly pasture rotation

Hens moved to fresh pasture every week — rotating the land keeps it alive, diverse, and naturally rich with plant species.

Biodiversity & soil health

Farming in harmony with nature restores living soil and diverse plant species — improving the whole farm ecosystem.

The egg-mobile

At night the hens put themselves to bed in their egg-mobile — safe from predators, with doors opening automatically with sunlight.

Natural behaviour, always

Scratching, dust bathing, chasing flies, finding worms — the hens express their most natural behaviour every single day.
